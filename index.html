Basics of AI

Artificial Intelligence is the simulation of human intelligence processes by machines.

AI

Broad concept of machines acting intelligently.

Machine Learning

Systems that learn and improve from data without explicit programming.

Deep Learning

Complex neural networks processing vast amounts of unstructured data.

History Timeline

  • 1950s: Turing Test & Perceptrons
  • 1980s: Expert Systems
  • 2012: AlexNet wins ImageNet (Deep Learning Boom)
  • 2017: Transformer Architecture invented
  • 2022+: Generative AI Era (ChatGPT, Midjourney)

Machine Learning

Algorithms that parse data, learn from that data, and then apply what they've learned to make informed decisions.

Supervised

Trained on labeled datasets.

Example: Spam detection, Price prediction

Unsupervised

Finds hidden patterns in unlabeled data.

Example: Customer segmentation, Anomaly detection

Reinforcement

Learns by trial and error using rewards/penalties.

Example: Robotics, Game AI (AlphaGo)

Deep Learning Architectures

Transformer Architecture

The backbone of modern LLMs (like GPT, Claude). It uses to weigh the importance of different words in a sentence, processing data in parallel rather than sequentially. This is what gives AI its human-like text generation capabilities.

Diffusion Models

The engine behind Midjourney, DALL-E, and Sora. It works by taking an image, systematically adding Gaussian noise until it's static, and then training a neural network to reverse the process—denoising random pixels into a sharp, newly generated image.

Natural Language Processing (NLP)

Bridging the gap between human communication and computer understanding.

Sentiment Analysis

Determining the emotional tone behind a series of words. Used constantly by brands monitoring social media.

Machine Translation

Translating text or speech from one language to another with contextual accuracy (e.g., DeepL, Google Neural MT).

Information Extraction

Automatically pulling specific data (names, dates, entities) from unstructured blocks of text.

Computer Vision

Enabling systems to derive meaningful information from digital images, videos, and other visual inputs.

  • Facial Recognition Systems

How AI Works.

Decoding the neural pathways that transform raw data into intelligent synthesis.

1. Data Ingestion

AI systems ingest massive datasets from text, images, and logic trees. This forms the foundational knowledge base, essentially feeding "experience" to the computational brain.

2. Neural Processing

Using deep learning algorithms, the raw data is filtered through millions of parameters. The system identifies complex patterns, relationships, and statistical probabilities.

3. Predictive Output

When queried, the trained model algorithmically predicts the most logical sequence of text, pixels, or code. The result is human-like comprehension and generative creation.

What is Artificial Intelligence?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the simulation of human intelligence in machines. AI systems learn from data, identify patterns, make decisions, and improve over time — often outperforming humans in specific tasks.

From voice assistants like Siri to self-driving cars, AI is no longer science fiction — it is the engine powering the 21st century economy, healthcare, education, and creativity.

Learning Systems Pattern Recognition Decision Making

Types of AI

Narrow AI (ANI)

AI that excels at one specific task. Examples: ChatGPT, Google Translate, Face Recognition.

General AI (AGI)

Hypothetical AI that matches human-level intelligence across all domains. Still in research.

Super AI (ASI)

Theoretical AI surpassing human intelligence in every domain. The future frontier.

Machine Learning

ML trains algorithms on data so they can make predictions without explicit programming. The model improves as it sees more data.

  • Supervised Learning
  • Unsupervised Learning
  • Reinforcement Learning

Deep Learning

Deep Learning uses neural networks with many layers. It powers image recognition, speech synthesis, and generative AI at scale.

  • Convolutional Neural Nets
  • Recurrent Neural Nets
  • Transformer Architecture

Large Language Models

LLMs like GPT-4, Claude, and Gemini are trained on vast text data to understand and generate human language with remarkable fluency.

  • Text Generation
  • Code Writing
  • Reasoning & Analysis
